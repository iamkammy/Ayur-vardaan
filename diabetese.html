What is Diabetes Mellitus?

It is a kind of ‘Metabolic Disorder’ in which the blood has high sugar levels for prolonged periods. This will lead to symptoms like frequent urination, thirst, and hunger. If left untreated this may lead to serious complications like- cardiovascular disease, stroke, chronic kidney disease, foot ulcers and serious damage to eyes.


You must learn about the terms and things below to understand DM in a better way.

- Glucose : It is the basic building block of all carbohydrates and is the main energy source for all living organisms. They have a lower tendency to react with amine groups of proteins. This is called- glycation. Glycation either impairs or destroys the protein structure. When you eat something it is broken down into glucose and then the glucose is used to release energy. Excess glucose is stored by various metabolic activities, in the body.

- Pancreas and Pancreatic islets : Pancreas is one of the ‘Vital Organs’ of our body and is located in the abdomen. It helps in the digestion of the food you eat. At the same time it regulates the blood sugar levels in your body by releasing two hormones- Insulin and Glucagon. Both these hormones work in opposite directions. When the blood sugar levels are high in body Insulin is released to regulate. Similarly, when the blood sugar levels are low in the body Glucagon is released to regulate the same. Both the hormones are produced in ‘islet cells (islets of Langerhans)

- Insulin : As mentioned, it is a ‘peptide hormone’ and is produced by the islet cells in the pancreas. It controls the breakdown and synthesis of carbohydrates, fats and proteins in the body. When it is released in the blood it therefore absorbs the glucose and forms protein or fats in the liver or skeletal muscles. When the insulin levels are low in blood or they are ineffective, then the other hormone- Glucagon is released which promotes breakdown of fats from the body into blood stream.

If insulin is not produced in the body as per the desired quantity then a metabolic disorder happens and this leads to three major kind of DM.0

  1. A. When the Islet Cells are destroyed: Due to autoimmune reaction (Self-Destruction) beta cells of islet cells in Pancreas are destroyed and hence insulin cannot be produced. As a result the sugar levels in blood can no longer be checked and becomes very high. This type of DM is termed as – Type-1 DM. It can also be termed as – IDDM (insulin-dependent Diabetes Mellitus). This type of DM can be managed ONLY with Insulin administration from outside.

  • B. When Cells are non-sensitive to Insulin: When either poor secretion of insulin in body happens or when the cells fail to respond to insulin. The most common cause of this type is sedentary lifestyle, or excessive stressor excessive body weight. This type is called- Type-2 DM or NIDDM (Non-Insulin dependent Diabetes Mellitus). Type-2 DM can be treated with medication with or without insulin.

  • C. During Pregnancy: A third form of DM happens during pregnancy when the blood sugar levels become very high, without any previous history. It is called as – Gestational Diabetes.     Complications arising from DM are :

    1. Damage to blood vessels, hence higher chances of cardiovascular diseases. 75% of deaths in diabetics are due to Coronary Artery Disease.

    4. Miniscule nerve endings get damaged due to prolonged, untreated, DM resulting into numbness, tingling, pain, in skin and foot. The muscles may loss the mass and can cause-muscle atrophy and eventually amputation of foot.
